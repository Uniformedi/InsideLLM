Scenario C's $50k 5-year labor savings (SRE fraction drops from 0.15 → 0.11 FTE) are eaten many times over by EC2 + storage + egress + AWS Support lines that together add ~$628k vs the on-prem baseline. Virtualization pays when the organization's hardware risk profile or data-residency requirements outweigh the gap — not in raw dollars.

7. Sensitivity + hidden costs

  • Usage 2× heavier than modeled — Anthropic API roughly doubles; Scenario A lands at ~$1.49M over 5 years (still wins vs A base at 2× use).
  • SRE not at 0.15 FTE — If Organization can't secure 0.15 FTE at the loaded rate used here, labor could be 1.5–2× higher. The single biggest unmodeled labor risk.
  • Hidden one-time labor — RHEL port (~$8k Y1), pentest if annual (~$25k/yr external), DR drills (~$4k/yr). Adds ~17% to Scenario A TCO if all materialize.
  • Anthropic price moves — Historical trend is down; a 20% drop cuts every scenario's Y5 by ~$50k.
