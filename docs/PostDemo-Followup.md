# Post-Demo Follow-Up Playbook

**Target:** Parent Organization portfolio leadership + Organization Compliance Manager
**Demo date:** 2026-05-12
**Window:** 48 hours after demo

This is the motion that converts demo momentum into a signed agreement, or
at minimum an inbound design-partner conversation. The bar for the first 48
hours is high: every hour after the demo, mindshare decays.

---

## T+2h — Immediate (same day)

**Before leaving the room / ending the call:**

- [ ] Confirm attendees. Write down everyone present + their role.
- [ ] Note any specific objection, concern, or "interesting if…" statement — verbatim.
- [ ] Identify the economic buyer and the champion. They are rarely the same person.
- [ ] Ask explicitly: *"What would you need to see to move this forward?"*
  Capture the answer word-for-word.
- [ ] Propose the next meeting on the spot. Two weeks is too long.
- [ ] Hand out printed **Executive-Summary.html** copies. Point out the contact line.

**Within 2 hours, before memory fades:**

- [ ] Write a private debrief: what landed, what didn't, who was engaged vs. checked out.
- [ ] Flag any technical question you answered uncertainly — research it before the follow-up email.
- [ ] Triage the attendee list: who gets the follow-up email, who gets a personal text, who gets nothing.

---

## T+6h — The day-of email

Send within 6 hours of the demo ending — before they go home, while the
memory is fresh.

**Subject line** (short, action-oriented):

```
InsideLLM follow-up — materials + proposed next step
```

**Template:**

```
[Name],

Thank you for the time today. A few quick things while it's fresh:

1. Materials attached — the Executive Summary, full Features List, and
   the Compliance Map specifically covering [FDCPA / HIPAA / the
   regulations they mentioned]. The Demo-Runbook is there too if your
   team wants to reproduce what we walked through.

2. One clarification on [the specific question someone raised]:
   [direct answer]. If you want more depth, I'm happy to put you in
   touch with [the technical detail / the policy source file / the
   audit verification mechanism].

3. Proposed next step: a 45-minute working session with [your
   compliance lead / your infra lead] within the next two weeks.
   Agenda would be: (a) your specific regulatory surface,
   (b) what a design-partner engagement for Organization would look
   like, (c) what a Wave-1 rollout across 2–3 portfolio companies
   would timeline to.

Calendar link: [link]. If those times don't work, reply with three
windows that do.

A direct question: is there a specific objection or constraint I
should be addressing before we go further? Better to name it now
than work around it.

— Dan
```

**Attachments:**
- `Executive-Summary.html` (or PDF export)
- `FeaturesList.html`
- `Compliance-Map.html` — but annotated or filtered to the regulations
  most relevant to Parent Organization's industries
- `Pricing.html` — only if pricing came up naturally; otherwise hold for later

**Do not attach:**
- SWOT analysis (internal)
- Opportunities list (internal)
- Competitors sheet (internal)
- Roadmap (share only after a clear signal of interest)

## T+24h — Stakeholder triage

Within 24 hours of demo day:

- [ ] Call the champion personally — not email. Short, ~15 min. Check the
  room temperature. "How did it land with [economic buyer]?"
- [ ] If a technical evaluator asked sharp questions: send them a follow-up
  email with one specific technical artifact (e.g., a rego file they can
  read, a link to the SESSION-RESUME.md for depth).
- [ ] If the Compliance Manager asked pointed questions: offer a 30-minute
  compliance-only deep dive as a separate meeting. Different agenda than
  the executive conversation.
- [ ] Update the pipeline CRM / tracking sheet with: confirmed attendees,
  stated objections, proposed next meeting, probability assessment.

---

## T+48h — The commitment test

By 48 hours:

- [ ] A concrete next meeting is on the calendar. If not, the demo did not
  convert — pivot to plan B (see below).
- [ ] One specific follow-up deliverable promised (document, reference,
  call) is delivered. On time, or early.
- [ ] The champion has repeated back to you — in their own words — why this
  matters for Parent Organization. If they can't, the story didn't stick
  and you need to sharpen the narrative for the next contact.

---

## Plan B — if the demo didn't convert

Not every demo lands. If by T+48h there's no confirmed next meeting:

- [ ] Send a low-key "no pressure" follow-up: *"Understood the timing
  isn't right. Let me know if anything shifts. In the meantime, here's
  something I think you'll find useful regardless."* Attach one
  content-rich artifact (a compliance whitepaper, a conference talk
  recording) that isn't a sales pitch.
- [ ] Flag for a quarterly re-engage. Put a calendar reminder for
  2026-08-12 to reach out again with a v3.2 update.
- [ ] Debrief honestly: was the objection real (they can't buy) or
  addressable (we haven't yet answered their real concern)? Log it.
- [ ] Ask the champion for a warm intro to someone else in their network
  who might be a better fit. Champions who can't buy often know people
  who can.

## The 14-day conversion plan (if demo converts)

### Days 1–3
- Day-of email sent
- Personal call with champion
- All follow-up deliverables prepared

### Days 4–7
- Working session with compliance lead + infra lead
- Scope a design-partner agreement: what Wave 1 looks like, commercial terms,
  SLA, reference-customer commitments
- Prepare draft MSA + SOW for review

### Days 8–14
- Legal / procurement process begins
- Technical diligence: provide access to source, architecture review sessions,
  SOC 2 readiness memo
- Scope deployment timeline: typical 4–6 weeks for a Wave-1 tenant
- Agree on signing date

---

## Boundary conditions — do not cross

- **Do not promise what's not in the roadmap.** If asked about v5 features,
  say "not currently on the roadmap; what would drive that for you?"
- **Do not oversell certifications.** SOC 2 is in progress, not held.
  Compliance alignment ≠ certification. Use the language precisely.
- **Do not discount aggressively in the first conversation.** Design-partner
  pricing is a lever, but it's a lever for a meaningful commitment — not a
  first-meeting concession.
- **Do not speak for Anthropic, OpenAI, Microsoft.** Model-provider SLAs
  and policies are the customer's direct relationship with those vendors,
  not ours.
- **Do not leak other customers' names or deployment details.** Reference
  conversations require explicit permission.

---

## The internal artifact

After the 48-hour window closes — win or lose — write a post-mortem:

- What happened in the room that the demo runbook didn't predict?
- Which talking point landed hardest? Which fell flat?
- Which question was unexpected, and what's the right answer next time?
- What does this update in the SWOT, Competitors, or Q&A anticipation sheet?

Feed the learnings back into the demo kit. Every demo makes the next one
better.
