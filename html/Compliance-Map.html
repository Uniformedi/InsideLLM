InsideLLM — Regulatory Compliance Map

How the platform enforces each in-scope regulation, which mechanism does the work, and what the platform does not claim.

Version 3.1.0 Cataloged 2026-04-22 Classification Internal / Uniformedi LLC

Design principle: compliance is enforced by machine-readable policy, not by prompt engineering. Every regulation below maps to one or more of four concrete enforcement surfaces. Failures are logged to an immutable audit chain and verifiable by the customer's auditor directly against the running system.

Debt Collection & Financial Services

Primary focus of InsideLLM's US Collections compliance story

FDCPA — Fair Debt Collection Practices Act

15 U.S.C. § 1692 et seq. · CFPB + state AG enforcement
DomainDebt collection communications with consumers — time, place, frequency, tone restrictions; validation-notice obligations; false / misleading representations prohibited.
Applies toThird-party debt collectors and, by several state laws, first-party creditors.
InsideLLM controls
  • OPA bundle configs/opa/policies/profiles/tier_fdcpa_regulated.rego — denies calls/callbacks outside §1692c(a)(1) permitted hours (8:00–21:00 consumer-local); validation-window witness requirement
  • Approval-gated send_letter action — consumer-facing communication cannot execute without a human approver
  • Hash-chained audit captures every dispute handling action for CFPB data-request response
  • DLP scanning prevents disclosure of unrelated consumer data in multi-account contexts
LimitationsPolicy correctness depends on the customer's own action definitions and tenant configuration; InsideLLM enforces the policy the customer publishes.

FCRA — Fair Credit Reporting Act

15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq. · CFPB + FTC enforcement
DomainConsumer credit reporting accuracy; dispute rights; permissible purposes; re-investigation obligations.
InsideLLM controls
  • Governance Framework controls documented in AI_Governance_Framework.md — tier-1 material decisions require human oversight and bias audits
  • Industry policy overlay available for credit-reporting-adjacent use cases
  • Full audit trail for every AI-driven decision that touches consumer credit data
LimitationsInsideLLM does not itself report to CRAs; it enforces controls on AI systems that may inform reporting decisions.

ECOA — Equal Credit Opportunity Act / Regulation B

15 U.S.C. § 1691 et seq. · 12 CFR Part 1002
DomainProhibits credit discrimination on prohibited bases (race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age, receipt of public assistance).
InsideLLM controls
  • Governance Framework requires bias audits for tier-1 (material consumer decision) AI applications
  • Hash-chained audit provides evidentiary record for disparate-impact analysis
  • OPA scope rules prevent AI agents from accessing prohibited-basis data fields unless explicitly authorized
LimitationsBias auditing is a process the customer performs against the audit record; platform provides data and controls, not the bias analysis itself.

CFPB Regulation F

12 CFR Part 1006 · CFPB enforcement
DomainImplements FDCPA with specific limits on call frequency (7-in-7 rule), email/text consent, and validation-notice content.
InsideLLM controls
  • OPA rules extending tier_fdcpa_regulated enforce call-frequency predicates
  • Consent-presence check before any text/email communication action fires
  • Validation-notice witness obligation in the OPA bundle
  • Hash-chained audit surfaces the 7-in-7 count over any window for CFPB examination

TCPA — Telephone Consumer Protection Act

47 U.S.C. § 227 · FCC + FTC enforcement
DomainRestrictions on calls to mobile numbers, autodialer use, pre-recorded messages, time-of-day limits, consent requirements.
InsideLLM controls
  • OPA rule denies agent-scheduled calls without consent record present in input
  • Time-of-day predicates enforced by the same permitted-hours rule as FDCPA
  • Full audit trail of consent state at time of call scheduling

GLBA — Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act

15 U.S.C. § 6801 et seq. · FTC + bank regulators
DomainFinancial institution customer-information privacy: Safeguards Rule (data security), Privacy Rule (disclosure limits), Pretexting Rule.
InsideLLM controls
  • DLP blocks financial-data patterns (account numbers, SSNs) in outbound AI responses unless explicitly authorized
  • OPA scope rules enforce purpose-limited access to customer-information fields
  • Hash-chained audit supports Safeguards Rule incident-response documentation
  • Per-user SSO + rate limits enforce access-control elements of the Safeguards Rule
NoteAI_Governance_Framework.md recognizes that the organization's existing GLBA compliance satisfies overlapping data-privacy obligations; AI-specific controls apply where GLBA does not address unique AI risks.

SOX — Sarbanes-Oxley Act

15 U.S.C. § 7241 et seq. · SEC enforcement
DomainInternal controls over financial reporting; segregation of duties; audit trail retention.
InsideLLM controls
  • OPA bundle tier_financial_regulated applies to financial-reporting-adjacent agents
  • Approval queue enforces segregation of duties — action initiator cannot be the same identity as the approver
  • Hash-chained audit provides the retention + tamper-evident log required by §302/§404
LimitationsPlatform does not substitute for SOX §404 assessment by management or attestation by the external auditor; it provides the control environment and the evidence.

Healthcare

HIPAA — Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act

45 CFR Parts 160, 162, 164 · HHS Office for Civil Rights enforcement
DomainProtected Health Information (PHI) — minimum-necessary use, access controls, audit trails, breach notification, Business Associate Agreement (BAA) obligations.
Applies toCovered entities (providers, payers, clearinghouses) and their Business Associates.
InsideLLM controls
  • DLP blocks PHI patterns (medical record numbers, diagnoses, prescription data) on inbound and outbound traffic
  • OPA bundle tier_hipaa_regulated enforces minimum-necessary access per agent scope
  • Model metadata baa_models — only models under a BAA with the covered entity are callable for PHI-containing requests
  • Hash-chained audit satisfies §164.312(b) audit-control requirement
  • On-prem deployment means PHI never leaves the customer's network boundary when using Ollama
LimitationsCustomer is responsible for ensuring their model provider (Anthropic, OpenAI) has executed a BAA when PHI flows to cloud models. InsideLLM enforces the routing decision; it does not execute the BAA.

Education

FERPA — Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act

20 U.S.C. § 1232g · U.S. Department of Education
DomainStudent educational records — disclosure restrictions, consent requirements, directory-information exceptions.
InsideLLM controls
  • OPA industry policy overlay for FERPA-covered agents
  • DLP can be configured with FERPA-specific patterns (student IDs, grades)
  • Audit trail supports disclosure-log obligations
LimitationsIndustry template is a starting point; institutional configuration of allowed collections and disclosure rules is the customer's responsibility.

Payment Systems

PCI-DSS — Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard

PCI Security Standards Council · contractually enforced by card networks
DomainCardholder data protection (not a law, but contractually binding on merchants and processors).
InsideLLM controls
  • DLP detects and blocks/redacts payment card number patterns (PAN, CVV) in any direction
  • OPA rules can enforce that no agent may retrieve cardholder data from a knowledge collection
  • Network isolation (on-prem deployment) supports Requirement 1 (firewall configuration)
LimitationsPCI-DSS compliance is a scope and process question for the overall cardholder-data environment; InsideLLM can be one component but does not of itself put an organization in scope for a Report on Compliance (RoC).

Cross-Cutting

FTC Act § 5 — Unfair or Deceptive Acts or Practices (UDAP)

15 U.S.C. § 45 · Federal Trade Commission
DomainProhibits unfair or deceptive practices in commerce. FTC has applied this to AI-generated deceptive claims, dark patterns, and undisclosed automation.
InsideLLM controls
  • Humility alignment requires that AI outputs disclose their AI nature when materially relevant
  • Governance framework documents the AI-generated content disclosure policy
  • Audit trail provides evidence of what was generated and when
  • DLP prevents AI from fabricating account-specific facts it does not have grounding for

Voluntary Frameworks & Standards

NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)

National Institute of Standards and Technology · voluntary
DomainGovern · Map · Measure · Manage — a risk-based framework for AI system lifecycle governance.
Alignment
  • Govern: Governance Framework doc defines roles, AI Ethics Officer, materiality tiers
  • Map: Tier classification (tier 1 material / tier 2 operational / tier 3 routine) maps AI context + impact
  • Measure: Hash-chained audit + Langfuse telemetry provides measurement surface
  • Manage: OPA enforcement, approval queues, and incident procedures provide management actions
NoteSafe-harbor alignment documented; not a third-party certification.

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — AI Management System

International Organization for Standardization · voluntary · certifiable
DomainManagement system standard for responsible AI development and use; aligned with ISO/IEC 27001 structure.
Alignment
  • Governance Hub + change management satisfy management-system process requirements
  • AI Governance Framework doc addresses policy, roles, risk assessment
  • Audit trail satisfies record-retention clauses
NoteISO/IEC 42001 certification by an accredited body is on the roadmap; current alignment is control-level, not audited.

What we do NOT claim

Be explicit about unheld certifications and open compliance questions

  • SOC 2 Type I / II — not currently held; planned for next quarter.
  • HITRUST CSF — not currently held; relevant for HIPAA-heavy customers.
  • ISO/IEC 27001 — not currently held.
  • ISO/IEC 42001 — control-level alignment only; no accredited certification.
  • FedRAMP Moderate / High — not currently authorized; relevant for federal customers.
  • EU AI Act — Uniformedi LLC has not published a formal conformity-assessment position. For high-risk AI systems deployed in the EU after the Act's 2026/2027 rollout, customers must perform their own conformity assessment; InsideLLM provides controls that would typically factor into such an assessment.
  • State AI laws — CO SB24-205 (Colorado AI Act), CA AB-2013 (Generative AI Training Data Transparency), NYC Local Law 144 (automated employment decision tools), and others have entered force or will during 2025–2026. InsideLLM's controls assist compliance but do not of themselves establish it.
  • Sector-specific sub-regulations — e.g., 23 NYCRR Part 500 (NY DFS cybersecurity), MA 201 CMR 17.00, Washington My Health My Data Act — customer must assess applicability.

How to verify compliance claims

Every claim in this document is verifiable without vendor involvement:

  1. Inspect OPA policy files — read configs/opa/policies/profiles/*.rego directly. The files are the single source of truth. Test offline: opa test ./configs/opa/policies/.
  2. Query the hash-chained audit — GET /governance/api/v1/audit/verify walks the chain and reports any broken links. A customer auditor can run this against production.
  3. Review DLP regex patterns — configs/litellm/callbacks/dlp_guardrail.py plus dlp_* Terraform variables declare every pattern matched.
  4. Review the Governance Framework — AI_Governance_Framework.md documents the policy posture including the formal AI definition (15 U.S.C. § 9401), tier-based materiality, and regulatory mapping.
  5. Verify model routing — LiteLLM key metadata declares which models each agent key can call. For HIPAA / BAA cases, baa_models restricts routing at the gateway.

If a claim here cannot be verified by the customer's auditor against running system state, it should not be in this document.
