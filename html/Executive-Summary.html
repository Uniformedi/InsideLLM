The Problem

Enterprises adopting AI face three linked risks that compound rather than cancel: data leakage (employees pasting PII/PHI/credentials into AI chat with no guardrails), cost explosion (unmanaged API usage hitting thousands of dollars within hours), and regulatory exposure (no audit trail of what was sent, to whom, under what authority). Every existing AI gateway solves one of those at a time, and all of them move customer data to a vendor cloud. Regulated industries — debt collection, healthcare, financial services, legal, insurance — cannot accept any of those trade-offs.

The Solution

InsideLLM is a self-hosted, on-premises AI gateway that governs every AI interaction with machine-readable policy, tamper-evident audit, and per-tenant sovereignty. A single terraform apply deploys the complete stack on Windows Hyper-V; a single PowerShell script on WSL2. No cloud dependency.

  • Policy-as-code compliance. OPA rego bundles enforce FDCPA, HIPAA, GLBA, SOX, PCI-DSS, FERPA — fails closed.
  • DLP at the gateway. PII, PHI, credentials, payment data blocked or redacted inline, inbound and outbound.
  • Hash-chained audit. SHA-256 chain on every governance event. Verifiable by customer's auditor directly.
  • Model-agnostic. Anthropic Claude, OpenAI, local Ollama — routed by policy, not vendor.

Differentiators

  • Stays on-premises. No Azure, no AWS, no Vertex required. Data never leaves the customer's network boundary unless the customer chooses a cloud model.
  • Full integrated stack. Gateway + chat UI (Open WebUI) + RAG + document generation (DocForge) + admin portal + multi-tenant fleet. Competitors ship one layer; InsideLLM ships all of them together.
  • Unique alignment IP. The Humility policy implements the SAIVAS framework from Uniform Gnosis, Vol. I. Mandatory baseline, four enforcement layers, cannot be disabled. Published as MIT at github.com/uniformedi/humility-guardrail.
  • Fleet-ready. One Governance Hub rolls up every instance in a portfolio for cross-tenant compliance visibility without cross-tenant data flow.
