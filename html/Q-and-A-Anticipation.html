Technical

What AI models does it support?
Any OpenAI-compatible model. Claude family (Opus 4.7 today), OpenAI GPT family, and local open-source via Ollama — all interchangeable. The demo runs claude-opus-4-7; HIPAA tenants can restrict to BAA-covered model sets.
What's the OPA decision latency at production scale?
P99 under 20ms in production. The demo VM is single-core so you'll see slightly higher. OPA runs in-process to the LiteLLM callback, not a network hop.
What happens if the model provider is down?
LiteLLM's routing tier supports fallback — you can configure Claude → OpenAI → local Ollama as a fallback chain. If all configured models are down, requests fail with a clear error. Governance policies still fire locally regardless of model availability.
How do you prevent prompt injection from rewriting the agent's manifest or policy?
Agent manifests are server-side data, not prompts. OPA policy input is constructed by the LiteLLM callback from request metadata; the model's output never feeds back into policy construction. Even a successful prompt injection can't escape the guardrail — as the Turn 3 demo shows.
Does it support function-calling / tools / MCP?
Yes. Tools are declared in the tenant action catalog and enforced through OPA at invocation time. MCP wiring is roadmapped for v3.4.
Can agents call other agents?
Not in the current release. Each agent is scoped to its own manifest. Multi-agent orchestration is an option for v4 if there's demand.
Does it support streaming responses?
Yes — LiteLLM passes streaming through. DLP outlet scanning happens on chunk boundaries and can redact mid-stream.
How do updates work without downtime?
Watchtower handles container patching on individual instances with brief restart. For fleet-wide upgrades, roll instance-by-instance via Governance Hub. True zero-downtime HA (active/active) is on the v4 roadmap.
How is data stored on the host?
PostgreSQL volume at /opt/InsideLLM/data/postgres, Redis in-memory with AOF persistence, all behind the Docker bridge 172.28.0.0/16. Nothing exposed to the host except Nginx 80/443 and LiteLLM 4000 (optionally).

Who holds the Anthropic BAA if we use Claude for PHI?
The customer holds the BAA directly with Anthropic. InsideLLM enforces the routing — only BAA-approved models are callable for PHI-tagged agents via the baa_models manifest field. We provide the control plane; you hold the vendor agreement.
What about the EU AI Act?
High-risk obligations phase in starting August 2026. Our controls align with most Annex III / Article 13-14 requirements. We're drafting a formal Conformity Profile whitepaper for v3.3. For deployments in the EU, customers perform the conformity assessment; we provide the controls and documentation.
How do we prove to CFPB that policy X was active on date Y?
The hash-chained audit captures every policy version change with a SHA-256 chain. One query returns the active policy at any timestamp, with a verifiable chain back to genesis. No log-forensics required.
Can we disable Humility / SAIVAS alignment?
No. It's the mandatory baseline — enforced at four layers including the LiteLLM callback that runs regardless of OPA availability. Industry overlays (FDCPA, HIPAA, etc.) are optional and feature-flagged; Humility is not.
What data leaves our network?
Only what you send to the model provider. If you route to Anthropic or OpenAI, prompts and completions flow to those providers per their privacy terms. If you use Ollama locally, nothing leaves at all. Audit logs, user data, spend tracking — all stay on your PostgreSQL.
What happens if OPA fails?
Fails closed. Any OPA error results in request denial. Defense in depth: the LiteLLM Humility guardrail callback also evaluates core rules locally in Python when OPA is unavailable, so policy enforcement continues even during OPA outages for the Humility baseline.
How are policy changes audited?
Policy updates go through the Governance Hub change-management workflow with supervisor approval. Every change lands in the hash-chained audit with the approver identity, timestamp, and diff. Tenant-policy changes are tracked separately from platform-policy changes.

Competitive

Why not ChatGPT Enterprise or Copilot for Enterprise?
ChatGPT has no per-tenant guardrail tiers, no policy-as-code, no hash-chained audit, no on-prem data plane. We don't compete with the model — we own the governance layer. Copilot is IDE-focused; we govern every AI use.
Why not AWS Bedrock or Azure AI Foundry?
Both are excellent if you're committed to that hyperscaler. We win when data sovereignty matters (true on-prem), when you want model portability (Claude + OpenAI + Ollama interchangeable), or when your regulatory surface needs industry-specific OPA bundles that the hyperscalers don't provide out-of-box.
Why not Portkey or Helicone?
Those are SaaS gateways — your data passes through their cloud. We're on-prem. They also focus on routing + observability; we add DLP + OPA + hash-chained audit + full integrated front-end. Different tool class.
What's unique about Humility / SAIVAS?
The SAIVAS framework is published IP from Uniform Gnosis, Vol. I — it's an explicit alignment ethics specification, not a vendor-specific filter. It's enforced at four layers (prompt, guardrail, OPA, pipeline), mandatory, and cannot be disabled. No other AI platform has a published, specification-backed alignment baseline of this kind.
Isn't this just LiteLLM with guardrails bolted on?
LiteLLM is the gateway layer — yes, we use it, and it's an excellent piece of software. But the platform is the whole stack: OPA engine, tenant action catalog, Governance Hub, hash-chained audit, SAIVAS alignment, fleet management, industry policy bundles, DLP, approval queues. LiteLLM alone gets you routing and budgets. InsideLLM gets you governance.
What about Guardrails AI, NeMo Guardrails?
Those are policy libraries — drop-in Python/JS frameworks for developers building their own stack. We ship the whole deployable system. If you're building your own platform and need a policy library, use theirs. If you want a turnkey governed AI platform, use ours.

Demo-specific

Why did the OPA deny in Turn 2 take a moment?
The demo VM is single-core. In production, P99 OPA decision latency is under 20ms. The deny you saw is the real policy evaluating — that latency is demo artifact, not representative.
Can we see the full audit chain, not just the last 5 entries?
Yes — strip the | jq '.[-5:]' and you get the complete chain. I trimmed for screen readability. The verification endpoint walks the whole chain and reports any broken links.
How would you handle a dispute in a jurisdiction outside the FDCPA, like a TCPA-only state consent requirement?
Apply the corresponding OPA policy overlay — tier_tcpa, or a custom bundle for the state's specific rules. The platform's pattern (base + industry overlay + tenant overlay) means a new jurisdiction adds a rego file, not a platform change.
What if the agent were asked to do something that violates multiple policies?
All applicable policies evaluate; the request is denied if any of them denies. The deny_reasons field returns every triggered rule so the reviewer sees the complete picture. This is important for ECOA-style overlapping regulations.
Can we run the same demo against our own tenant and our own data?
Absolutely — that's a design-partner engagement we'd love to have. Typical timeline: 2-3 weeks to stand up a tenant with your data and run the dispute-handler scenario against real account data. Let's schedule a follow-up call within two weeks of today.
How would this handle 1,000 concurrent agents?
Per-instance: tens of concurrent agents are fine. Hundreds-to-thousands needs horizontal scaling — multiple InsideLLM instances in a fleet, Governance Hub central aggregation, and load-balanced LiteLLM. That's the v3.4 / v4 scaling work. For your 32-company portfolio, 32 instances with fleet management is the natural topology.
Can we use this for non-collections workflows — HR, legal research, general chat?
Yes. Any AI use in your organization can route through the platform. The Dispute Handler is a showcase for regulated-workflow governance; the same platform handles general chat (Tier 3 governance) and internal productivity AI (Tier 2) just as cleanly.
What if we want our General Counsel to write a custom policy?
Perfect use case. OPA policies are rego files — human-readable, version-controlled, reviewable in GitHub or the admin UI's policy editor. Our custom-policy-authoring service can partner with your GC team, or Professional-tier customers can author policies directly with our training material.
